Week 5:

Programmable Electronics

There were two assignments for week 5. They are the following:

  • Program a microcontroller to produce an output using at least one button for input. Include a conditional statement and a for() loop.
  • Think about and CAD material for the final project.

Because I am thinking of making an RC airplane for my final project, I decided to continue my work on flaps (yes, I know I have no creativity). I decided to switch over from the metro board this week to the feather. This change was mainly because the feather was smaller. The feather also has WIFI capabilities, but that was no of utmost concern.

Switching over to the feather was not too difficult. One significant difference was that analogWrite does not exist on the feather; instead, there is ledcWrite which is explained well here:

https://techexplorations.com/guides/esp32/begin/pwm/

I started the project by writing the code. Because I was still unfamiliar with ledcWrite despite reading the article, I installed a library that allowed me to analogWrite with the feather (or any other esp32 board). This is the link to that library:

https://www.arduino.cc/reference/en/libraries/esp32-analogwrite/
