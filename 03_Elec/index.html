Week 3:

Electronics and Tools

For week 3, the assignment had multiple parts. I have listed the requirements below:

  • Create a circuit
  • Use a multimeter to measure voltages in the circuit
  • Use Ohm's Law to determine the current through some part of the circuit
  • Use the Microcontroller Analog in (ADC) to measure the voltage of a potentiometer, or other voltage-divider-based sensor

I made three circuits. The first was just two lightbulbs that were in series with one another.


Next, I made a circuit that had three lightbulbs in series. The light obviously got dimmer. This happens because the resistance (lightbulbs are resistors) increased as the current remained the same. The voltage for each lightbulb therefore decreased.


To make the three lightbulbs the same brightness as two lightbulbs, two of the three lightbulbs must be in parallel. This results in equal voltage going through each lightbulb that is in series with the first.
