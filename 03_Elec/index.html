Electronics and Tools

For week 3, the assignment had multiple parts. I have listed the requirements below:

  • Create a circuit
  • Use a multimeter to measure voltages in the circuit
  • Use Ohm's Law to determine the current through some part of the circuit
  • Use the Microcontroller Analog in (ADC) to measure the voltage of a potentiometer, or other voltage-divider-based sensor

I made three circuits. The first was just two LEDs that were in series with one another.


Next, I made a circuit that had three LEDs in series. The brightness obviously got dimmer. This happens because the resistance (LEDs have internal resistance) increased as the current remained the same. The voltage for each LED therefore decreased.


To make the three LEDs the same brightness as two LEDs, two of the three LEDs must be in parallel. This results in equal voltage going through each LED that is in series with the first.
