Week 9:

Networking

The homework for week 9 is the following:

  • Control a board from a local wireless network.
  • Program a microcontroller to obtain and respond to information from the internet.

The two assignments are related, so I decided I would use the information from the internet to control the board.

The project I had in mind involved APIs and some other coding. I started with a sketch that we went over in class. This sketch pulled information from the national weather service. Right away, I changed the URL from the national weather service to RapidAPI, where I can pull information from the stock market.

It took a lot of time for me to figure out how to incorporate APIs that were not from the national weather service. I needed to add a header, and I had no idea how to do it. After some research, I ran across https://stackoverflow.com/questions/65619359/how-to-add-headers-to-http-request-in-arduino-ide, which taught me how to enter the API key and host. I needed only to pull the current stock price from the entire document. That's when I incorporated the ArdunioJson library that lets me cherrypick data.

The first time I ran the code, there was a lack of memory. After some research, I found out this error meant I needed to allocate more bytes for the program to run. I changed 10,000 bytes to 100,000 bytes. After I did that, everything worked fine.
